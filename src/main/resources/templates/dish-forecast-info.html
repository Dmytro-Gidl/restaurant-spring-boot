Forecast guide

Use these notes to choose a forecasting method. When there is little history, all models fall back to repeating the last month.

  • Holt‑Winters follows gradual trends and is the current default.
  • ARIMA (1,0,0) reacts quickly to swings up or down.
  • Auto‑ARIMA tries a few simple patterns and keeps the one that fits best.

MAPE (percentage error) and RMSE (typical miss) appear on the forecast page. Lower is better. If both show n/a, fewer than two months had orders.

At least three non‑zero months are needed before model differences become visible.

Example scenarios

  • Steady growth: enter 5, then 10, then 15 orders. Holt‑Winters keeps climbing, ARIMA trails slightly, and auto‑ARIMA may stick with the average if the trend is short.
  • Mean‑reverting demand: alternate high and low months around a baseline (20 then 5). ARIMA mirrors the swings, Holt‑Winters smooths them, and auto‑ARIMA chooses the simplest fit.
  • Sparse orders: record numbers only in a few scattered months. All models warn about missing history and show a flat line, telling you more data is needed.
